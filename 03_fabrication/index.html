After consulting with Bobby, I settled on a two-gear structure: a smaller gear attached to the oblong motor shaft powering a larger gear, which would carry the mobile. Using Fusion&rsquo;s Spur Gear tool, I designed gears that meshed properly and built a housing to secure both the gears and motor, projecting holes aligned with the motor&rsquo;s so I could fasten everything with nuts and bolts.</p> <img src="Gears.png" alt="Gears" class="center"> <p style="text-align: center;"><em>CAD model of motor, gears, and platform</em></p> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <a id="btn" href="Kinetic Sculpture Fusion.f3d" download>Kinetic Sculpture Fusion Files </a> </div> <p class="margin"> </p> <br></br> <h3>First Prototype and Fixes</h3> <p>My first laser-cut plywood prototype revealed several issues, which I iterated on:</p> <ul> <li><p class="p1"><strong>Large gear not suspended properly</strong></span> &rarr; Added spacers to hold it at the right vertical height to mesh with the small gear.</li> <li><p class="p1"><strong>Motor not sitting flush</strong></span> &rarr; Cut a square hole in the platform for the motor&rsquo;s protrusions so it could sit flat.</li> <li><p class="p1"><strong>Small gear hitting a motor nubbin</strong></span> &rarr; Elevated it with three stacked washers, lifting it higher on the z-axis.</li> <li><p class="p1"><strong>Motor shaft hole too tight</strong></span> &rarr; Initially cut to the exact size of the oblong motor shaft, it left no room for rotation. I drilled it out manually to give the shaft clearance.</li> </ul> <p>With these adjustments, the gears rotated together smoothly: exactly the validation I wanted at this stage of the process.</p> <div style="text-align: center;"> <video width="480" height="360" controls> <source src="IMG_4054.mp4" type="video/mp4"> Your browser does not support the video tag. </video> </div> <br></br> <h3>Ornaments &amp; Frame</h3> <p>Next, I created the mobile frame using a dowel and a small stick of wood.
